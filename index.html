About Us

About NSS Logo

The logo for the NSS has been designed based on the giant Rath Wheel of the world famous Konark Sun Temple (The Black Pagoda) situated in Orissa, India. The giant wheels of the Sun Temple portray the cycle of creation, preservation and release, and signify the movement in life across time and space. The eight bars in the wheel of NSS logo represent the 24 hours of a day. The red colour indicates that the volunteer is full of young blood that is lively, active, energetic and full of high spirit. The navy blue colour indicates the cosmos of which the NSS is tiny part, ready to contribute its share for the welfare of the mankind.

History of NSS

Dr. V.K.R.V. Rao ,On September 24, 1969, the then Union Education Minister Dr. V.K.R.V. Rao, launched the NSS programme in 37 universities involving 40000 students.
The motto of National Service Scheme is NOT ME BUT YOU
Principal elements of NSS: There are four principal elements in the NSS programme process; they are Students, Teachers, Community and the programme. 'Education through Service' is the purpose of the NSS. The ideological orientation of the NSS is inspired by the ideals of Mahatma Gandhiji's Centenary year in 1969. NSS is an extension dimension to the higher education system to orient the student youth to community service while they are studying in educational institutions. Since inception of the NSS in the year 1969, the number of students strength increased from 40,000 to over 3.8 million up to the end of March 2018.
